# Privacy Breach Runbook

## Canadian Law Obligations

### PIPEDA (s.10.1 — federal)

Step 1: Assess RROSH (Real Risk of Significant Harm)
Factors: sensitivity of data, cause of breach, number affected

Step 2 (if RROSH = YES):
- Report to Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (OPC)
  Timeline: "as soon as feasible" (no fixed day limit — document your internal target: recommend ≤72h of RROSH determination)
- Notify affected individuals: "as soon as feasible" (internal target: recommend ≤7 days of RROSH determination)
- Maintain breach records: minimum 24 months from breach date

### Alberta PIPA (provincial — applies to Alberta private-sector orgs)

Step 1: Assess RROSH

Step 2 (if RROSH = YES):
- Report to Office of the Information and Privacy Commissioner of Alberta (OIPC Alberta)
  Timeline: "without unreasonable delay"
- Notify affected individuals: "without unreasonable delay"

## Notification Templates

### Template A — Regulatory Notification (OPC / OIPC Alberta)

Subject: Notification of Privacy Breach — [Organization Name]
Body: Date of breach, date detected, nature of breach, personal information affected, number of individuals, steps taken to contain and mitigate, contact information.

### Template B — Individual Notification (plain language)

Subject: Important notice about your personal information
Body: What happened, what information was involved, what we are doing, what you can do, contact information.
